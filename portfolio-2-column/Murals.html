The Mural Project Arjun Chakravarthy

Overview:

First thing we had to do for. a mural was get the measurements of the wall. The measurements ended up being 265" by 95", which was then converted into 11"by 4". So I drew my Final design on a place that big. Before we did that, I had to decide what to draw. Since it was my first time doing a Mural, We decided to do some simple flowers. Believe me, I drew out an entire valley with trees, a river, and a sunrise, but that was way too complex. Instead, I drew that on a canvas. I ended up with a simple flower design with tthe occasional bee or cloud. Then I had to research the rocess of mural painting. I found out that acrylic paint was good enough. I also fund out that the wall needed to have a coat of primer attached to it. Only then can you draw the design onto the wall. I used grids to help this process. once we finished painting the place, we traced it with wall pens, and then coated it in varnish.

Buy:

From my research, I decided that we needed to buy the following items: One quart of each color paint, a quart of primer (we already had some at home), a paint roller, large paintbrushes, a clear varnish, wall markers, a yardstick, and painter's tape.

Prep:

To prepare the wall, we spent a full day painting the wall with the primer. We had already washed the wall, and it was hard work to paint the entire wall in the afternoon in the middle of summer, but we managed. Then we used the grid to draw the design onto the wall.
